About Barangay Lumbia

Named for the trees that once stood here

From forested slopes named for the palms that once grew here to the community of today, this is the story of Barangay Lumbia.

History

Early 1866, Lumbia was a desolate place. It was densely forested. Aside from the trees and the wild plants that usually abound, wood land palms of various varieties grow in abundance; some are Kalainog, Pugahan, Lubi-Lubi, Buli, Pitogo, Limbog, etc. The sturdiest, most imposing and enduring to climatic changes were the Lumbia Palms. These palms grew vigorously and stood stately alongside springs, streams, and creeks and under the big trees.

Due to this belief, no one ever dared to cut or destroy a part of the palm for fear that this might enrage the Engkantos and harm the populace. Since it was customary that women and children did most of their work in the farms, the produce was meager. During the summer months, the wild grasses withered and even small streams dried up.

Location

Lumbia sits in the southern reaches of Cagayan de Oro, within the city's 1st District (West). Its elevation gives it noticeably cooler air and wider views than the lowland barangays closer to the coast. It borders Dansolihon, Pagatpat, and Bayanga, with its southern edge meeting Barangay Ticala in Talakag, Bukidnon.

Tourist Spots

Hidden gems worth the trip

From forested ridges to riverside picnics, here's where to start exploring Barangay Lumbia.

Camarahan Ridge

Camarahan Ridge

Camarahan Ridge is a scenic destination near Barangay Lumbia known for its cool climate, beautiful mountain views, and peaceful surroundings. It is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and sightseeing.

Macambus Cave

Macambus Cave

Macahambus Cave and Gorge is a historical and natural attraction near Barangay Lumbia, Cagayan de Oro. It is known for its limestone cave, scenic views, forest trails, and its role in the Philippine-American War. It is a popular place for sightseeing and learning about local history.

Church

Church

The church in Barangay Lumbia, Cagayan de Oro, such as the Our Lady of the Miraculous Medal Parish Church, is a place of worship and a center for the Catholic community. It serves as a gathering place for prayers, religious celebrations, and community activities, reflecting the faith and culture of the residents of Lumbia.

Cabula River

Cabula River

Cabula River in Barangay Lumbia, Cagayan de Oro City is a peaceful natural attraction known for its clean flowing water, green surroundings, and relaxing atmosphere. It is a popular place for swimming, picnics, and enjoying nature.
